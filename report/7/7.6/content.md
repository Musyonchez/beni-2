# 7.6 Conclusion

The testing and evaluation phases played a vital role in validating the functionality, reliability, and scalability of the USIU Campus Navigator. These processes ensured the system met its operational goals—streamlining campus navigation, enabling intelligent facility discovery, and supporting real-time status tracking for all user roles.

Multiple testing methodologies were applied across the system lifecycle, including unit testing for individual components, integration testing for Google Maps and local data interactions, system testing for end-to-end workflows, and user acceptance testing (UAT) to verify that the platform aligns with stakeholder expectations. Manual validations on physical devices helped simulate realistic user interactions and edge cases, such as navigating without internet access or switching rapidly between tabs.

Evaluation extended beyond correctness to cover performance, usability, and stability. Performance benchmarks confirmed that the application initializes the map and filters search results within acceptable time limits (under 2 seconds). Usability assessments confirmed that the interface is intuitive for new students and visitors alike. Data handling and persistence mechanisms were reviewed to ensure that user favorites are retained reliably across sessions without data loss.

Ultimately, this rigorous testing and evaluation ensured that the USIU Campus Navigator is not only functionally complete but also resilient, user-friendly, and maintainable—ready for real-world deployment on campus and future scalability.

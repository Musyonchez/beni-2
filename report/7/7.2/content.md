# 7.2 System Testing

System testing is a critical phase in the software development lifecycle that involves evaluating the complete and integrated software product to ensure it complies with the specified requirements. Unlike unit or integration testing, which focus on individual components or modules, system testing validates the application as a unified whole in an environment that closely mimics real-world use. The primary objective is to verify that the system meets both functional specifications—what the system does, and non-functional requirements, how the system performs, such as security, speed, and reliability. This comprehensive evaluation is essential to identify and rectify any defects that may arise when different modules interact, ensuring the final deliverable is robust and ready for deployment.

For the USIU Campus Navigator, system testing played a pivotal role in validating the performance and reliability of the android application under realistic campus scenarios. This phase was designed to verify that the system's integrated modules—such as the interactive map rendering, facility search algorithms, secure user authentication, and route generation logic—operated seamlessly as a cohesive unit. The testing process involved installing the application on various Android devices to simulate the actual experience of students and faculty navigating the university grounds.

The objective was to assess the system's ability to meet specific non-functional requirements defined during the analysis phase. Emphasis was placed on validating Security (NFR2) to ensure safe access, Performance (NFR1) to guarantee rapid route generation, and Scalability (NFR5) to confirm the system can handle growth.

# CHAPTER ONE: INTRODUCTION

Universities are dynamic environments that host thousands of students, staff, and visitors daily. Navigating large campuses can often be confusing, especially for new students and first-time visitors unfamiliar with the layout. United States International University – Africa (USIU-A), as a leading institution of higher learning in the region, has a wide range of academic buildings, administrative offices, recreational facilities, and service points that can be challenging to locate.

While general navigation tools such as Google Maps offer effective street-level guidance, they lack the detail and customization required for campus-specific navigation. They do not provide floor-level directions, building descriptions, or service-specific details tailored to the unique environment of USIU. This gap creates a need for a dedicated campus navigation system that is user-friendly, precise, and tailored to the institution's context.

The emergence of mobile technologies and mapping platforms has made it possible to design applications that combine real-time positioning with location-specific data. Leveraging these technologies, this project seeks to develop a Campus Navigation and Mapping Application specifically designed for USIU-A to improve accessibility, save time, and enhance the user experience within the university.

# 2.3.2 University of Lagos (UNILAG) Campus Navigator, Nigeria

The University of Lagos (UNILAG) has implemented a semi-digital campus navigation solution that combines web-based maps and a mobile-accessible guide to help students and visitors locate academic departments, lecture halls, and facilities. The system uses geotagged locations and route indicators to support easy movement within the university's expansive environment. While the platform lacks real-time GPS integration, it represents a growing effort among African universities to digitize campus navigation and improve user experience. UNILAG's initiative illustrates the regional shift towards adopting campus mapping technologies in higher education institutions across Africa.

Figure 6 and 7 above show The UNILAG Campus Navigator exemplifies the progressive digital transformation taking place within African higher education institutions. Its integration of web-based maps with geotagged points offers users an organized overview of the university's large and complex layout. The use of route indicators allows students and visitors to visualize directions between key locations such as lecture halls, administrative offices, and residential areas. Although it operates without real-time GPS tracking, the system still provides a strong foundation for location-based services within the university context. This semi-digital approach demonstrates how institutions with limited technological infrastructure can still leverage accessible web tools to improve campus mobility.

The design of the UNILAG navigation system focuses on simplicity and functionality. Its clear labeling, clickable icons, and structured layout enable users to identify destinations without confusion. The platform's mobile compatibility ensures that users can access maps on smartphones, which aligns with the growing reliance on mobile devices among university students. This responsiveness makes it convenient for on-the-go users navigating the large UNILAG campus. Additionally, the inclusion of essential facilities such as libraries, cafeterias, hostels, and parking spaces enhances the system's practicality for both new and continuing users.

From a regional perspective, UNILAG's effort marks an important step toward digital modernization in African universities. It reflects how institutions are gradually embracing mapping technologies to improve user experience and campus accessibility. For the USIU Campus Navigation System, the UNILAG model offers insights into balancing innovation with feasibility. While advanced features such as live GPS tracking and real-time updates may come later, starting with geotagged locations, interactive icons, and mobile adaptability would create a strong and sustainable foundation. By building upon such regional innovations, USIU can design a navigation platform that is both technologically progressive and contextually relevant to African university environments.

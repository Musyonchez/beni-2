# 2.4.2 Strathmore University Smart Campus Map, Kenya

Strathmore University has adopted a smart campus approach that integrates digital mapping into its student information system. The system allows users to locate lecture rooms, departments, and essential facilities through an interactive interface accessible on mobile and desktop platforms. The university's initiative reflects a growing local trend toward integrating technology in campus management and student services. However, the system is limited in terms of real-time navigation and GPS-based guidance, creating an opportunity for USIU-A's campus navigation system to offer a more advanced, location-aware solution tailored to user movement within the university.

> [Figure 10: The Strathmore University Smart Campus Map — Google Maps overlay showing the Strathmore University campus boundary highlighted in blue]

Figure 10 above shows The Strathmore University Smart Campus Map represents one of the most forward-looking digital navigation initiatives within Kenya's higher education landscape. By integrating its mapping platform into the broader student information system, Strathmore demonstrates how technology can be embedded into academic and administrative functions to enhance convenience and connectivity. The map's interactive interface allows users to search for and identify buildings, departments, and service areas with ease. Its compatibility with both desktop and mobile devices further strengthens its accessibility, ensuring that users can obtain navigation support at any time and from any location. This integration of mapping and institutional data positions Strathmore as a pioneer in developing a more connected and technology-driven learning environment.

However, while the system's structure is user-friendly and technologically advanced compared to many local institutions, its static nature limits the full potential of location-based interaction. The absence of real-time navigation and GPS functionality means that users must rely primarily on visual orientation rather than live guidance. This restricts the system's usefulness in providing turn-by-turn directions or assisting new students and visitors in locating precise destinations across the campus. Despite these constraints, the platform remains an important milestone in Kenya's digital campus transformation journey, signaling a shift toward smart campus management systems that prioritize efficiency, accessibility, and digital inclusion.

For the USIU Campus Navigation System, the Strathmore model provides an excellent foundation to build upon. It illustrates the benefits of system integration, cross-platform accessibility, and intuitive design. However, it also reveals opportunities for innovation—specifically through real-time GPS guidance, route optimization, and interactive feedback features. By incorporating these advancements, the USIU system could go beyond static mapping to create a dynamic, location-aware solution that not only enhances campus mobility but also contributes to a smarter and more connected university ecosystem.

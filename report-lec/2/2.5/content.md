## Analysis

Table 1 above presents the strengths and weaknesses of various existing campus navigation systems, ranging from global solutions such as *MIT WhereIs* and *NUS NextBus*, to regional platforms like UCT Campus Maps and UNILAG Campus Navigator, and local initiatives including UoN Wayfinding Maps and Strathmore Smart Campus Map. Based on this evaluation, clear gaps and opportunities are visible in the current campus navigation landscape.

Global systems are highly advanced, featuring real-time mapping, integration with transport networks, and sophisticated data visualization. However, their complexity, high cost, and need for continuous technical support make them less practical for smaller or mid-sized institutions. Conversely, regional and local systems are simpler and more accessible, offering cost-effective solutions and localized relevance but lacking in real-time functionality, automation, and scalability.

This discrepancy highlights the need for a balanced, context-aware campus navigation solution that integrates the best features of global systems (like real-time mapping and data accuracy) while maintaining the simplicity and affordability of local platforms.

The proposed USIU-Africa Campus Navigation System aims to fill this gap by delivering a GPS-enabled, mobile-responsive platform designed for university environments. It emphasizes real-time location tracking, user-friendly interfaces, and accessibility for students, visitors, and staff. Unlike static maps or externally hosted solutions, this system will be modular, easy to update, and tailored to the specific layout and infrastructure of the campus.

By focusing on interactivity, accessibility, and localized design, the system will transform the campus experience—simplifying navigation, enhancing accessibility, and reducing time spent searching for facilities. This positions USIU-Africa as a leader in smart campus innovation within the region.

# 5.1.9 Wireframe

The wireframes for the Campus Navigation System serve as the main blueprint of the entire mobile app. They outline how each screen will look, what components will appear, and how users will move from one function to another. At this stage, the focus is not on colors or decoration but on structure and functionality. The wireframes help to ensure that features like search, navigation, location markers, and campus details are well-organized before actual coding begins.

Wireframing played a key role in transforming the idea of the app into a structured plan. Since the app aims to help students, staff, and visitors find their way around campus, it was important to visualize how users would move between pages like the login page, home screen, search bar, and map interface. The wireframes make it easy to see how each of these sections will connect and how users can quickly locate buildings, departments, or routes.

Before creating the diagrams, the main user journeys were mapped out in simple lists and flowcharts. For example, a user might open the app, sign in, tap the search bar, type the name of a lecture hall, and then view the route to reach it. These steps helped identify what pages were necessary and what elements needed to be included on each screen.

The initial wireframes were low-fidelity, meaning they were drawn using simple boxes, circles, and labels. These rough sketches helped to decide where to place major elements like navigation menus, icons, and buttons. For instance, the top of the screen might include a search field, the center area might display the interactive map, and the bottom part might hold shortcut buttons for quick access to key features. This stage helped create a clear structure that both designers and developers could understand.

> [Figure 22: Wireframe]

Figure 22 above represents the wireframe for the campus navigation system. The first page of the USIU Campus Navigation System wireframe focuses on the maps and search functionality, which serves as the central point of interaction for users. A prominently placed search bar allows students, staff, and visitors to type in the name of a building, department, or facility, with an auto-complete feature that suggests relevant locations as they type, thereby improving efficiency and reducing search time. The main area of the page is dedicated to an interactive digital map of the USIU-Africa campus, which allows zooming, panning, and selection of specific locations. When a user selects a facility, a pin marker appears on the map along with a pop-up card displaying details such as the name, function, and distance from the user's current location. Integration of GPS-based real-time tracking enables users to see their live position and follow a visual route toward their destination.

Additional features on this page include filters and layer controls that allow users to toggle between different categories of locations, such as academic buildings, libraries, cafeterias, or hostels. This design choice ensures that users can customize the map view according to their specific needs, making the system more versatile and user-centered. The interface emphasizes clarity and simplicity, ensuring that even first-time visitors can navigate the campus effectively while reducing cognitive load through intuitive design elements. Overall, this page establishes the foundation for a seamless navigation experience by combining interactive mapping, real-time location tracking, and user-friendly search functionality.

The second page presents different school facilities in a structured and organized layout. Facilities are grouped into categories such as academic buildings, administrative offices, student services, and recreational areas. Each facility is represented by a clickable icon or card that provides key information, including the facility name, purpose, and any operational details. This page enhances user exploration by allowing students and visitors to browse the campus visually rather than relying solely on search queries. Quick-access tabs or filters enable users to narrow down facilities by category, which improves usability and reduces the time needed to locate a desired destination. The design also emphasizes consistency in visual elements and labeling to ensure that users can quickly recognize different types of facilities at a glance.

The third page focuses on favorited facilities, offering users the ability to save frequently visited or important locations for quick access. Each favorited facility is displayed in a list or grid format with key details, such as distance from the user and a shortcut to navigation. Users can manage their favorites by adding or removing facilities, creating a personalized and efficient navigation experience. This feature supports daily campus routines and enhances convenience, especially for students who regularly move between lecture halls, libraries, and other frequently used spaces. Visual indicators, such as star icons or bookmarks, make the interface intuitive and allow users to identify their saved locations at a glance.

The fourth page is dedicated to emergency contacts, providing quick access to critical services such as campus security, medical assistance, and administrative support. Contact information is displayed prominently along with icons that represent each service, and a one-touch call or message feature is integrated to allow immediate communication in urgent situations. The design prioritizes accessibility, ensuring that users can locate emergency contacts quickly without navigating through multiple menus. Color coding, consistent iconography, and clear labeling enhance visibility and reduce response time during emergencies. This page emphasizes safety and preparedness, reinforcing the system's role as not only a navigation tool but also a resource for campus security and student wellbeing.

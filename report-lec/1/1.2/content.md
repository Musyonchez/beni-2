# 1.2 Problem Statement

Navigating a university campus is often a complex task, particularly for new students, staff members, and first-time visitors. United States International University – Africa (USIU-A) has a relatively large and diverse campus environment with multiple academic blocks, administrative offices, recreational facilities, student service centers, and specialized buildings. Despite the availability of signposts and orientation sessions, many individuals continue to experience difficulty in locating specific destinations within the institution. This challenge results in wasted time, missed appointments, and unnecessary frustration, which negatively impacts the overall campus experience.

Existing navigation solutions, such as Google Maps, offer only general outdoor navigation and do not provide detailed, campus-specific guidance. While these tools can direct a user to the university's main gate or external landmarks, they lack the precision to navigate within the campus boundaries effectively. They also do not provide contextual details such as building names, their functions, or services offered. For instance, while a user might be directed to the general location of the Student Center, they may still struggle to identify specific offices or departments within that building.

The absence of a dedicated campus navigation tool creates barriers not only for students but also for staff and external visitors. New students often find it difficult to identify lecture halls during their first weeks of study, while visitors attending seminars, workshops, or administrative appointments may arrive late due to confusion about where facilities are located. This inefficiency disrupts learning, service delivery, and general institutional operations.

There is, therefore, a clear need for a customized, user-friendly mobile navigation application designed specifically for the USIU-A campus. Such a system should not only allow users to search and locate buildings but also provide guided routes and essential details about each facility. Addressing this problem will significantly enhance accessibility, improve time management, and contribute to the broader goal of creating a smart, technology-driven campus environment.

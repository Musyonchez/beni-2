# 3.2 General Aims and Objectives

The general aim of this project is to design, develop, and evaluate a mobile-based campus navigation system specifically for United States International University – Africa (USIU-A). This initiative seeks to directly address the documented inefficiencies and challenges faced by students, staff, and visitors in locating facilities across the campus. By moving beyond traditional methods like static maps and physical signage, the project aims to provide a reliable, user-friendly, and campus-specific digital solution. Ultimately, this endeavor contributes to the broader strategic goals of enhancing campus accessibility and driving digital transformation, thereby positioning USIU-A as a forward-thinking, smart campus within the landscape of higher education.

A comprehensive review of existing literature will be conducted to establish a strong theoretical and practical foundation for this project. This survey will critically analyze prior research on mobile navigation systems, with a specific focus on their application within educational environments. It will investigate common technological approaches, such as the use of GPS, GIS, and beacon technology, and evaluate their respective strengths and limitations. Furthermore, the literature review will identify and synthesize the key usability principles, user interface (UI) design patterns, and user experience (UX) criteria that are most critical for the success of similar applications. This process will ensure the project is informed by established best practices and helps to clearly define the research gap that this specific, context-aware solution for USIU-A will fill.

The design and development of the application will be guided by a structured architectural framework to ensure robustness and usability. The system will be conceptualized with a clear separation between the backend and frontend components. The backend will be responsible for managing the core data, including detailed campus maps, building footprints, points of interest (POIs), and efficient pathfinding algorithms. The frontend, developed as a mobile application, will feature an intuitive interface designed for ease of use. Key functionalities to be implemented include interactive maps, a searchable directory of campus facilities, and step-by-step navigation instructions. The design process will prioritize a user-centered approach, ensuring the final product is tailored to the specific layout and needs of the USIU-A campus.

The final phase of the project will involve rigorous testing and evaluation of the developed prototype to validate its effectiveness and readiness for deployment. The testing regimen will encompass both technical performance and user acceptance. Technically, the application will be assessed for the accuracy of its navigation routes, the speed of its search functionality, and its overall stability. For user-centric evaluation, a group of representative users (students, staff, and visitors) will be recruited to participate in usability tests and provide feedback through structured surveys. Key metrics such as task completion time, error rates, and user satisfaction scores will be collected and analyzed. The insights gained from this comprehensive evaluation will be used to refine the application and demonstrate the extent to which it meets its core objective of improving campus navigation.

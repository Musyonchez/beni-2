# 8.4 Future Work

While the current version of the USIU Campus Navigator meets its core objectives of providing reliable outdoor navigation and facility information, there are several opportunities to expand its capabilities and deepen its integration with campus life in future iterations. One key area for enhancement is the development of Indoor Navigation. Currently, the application guides users to the entrance of a building. Future work will involve mapping the interiors of complex multi-story structures, such as the Library and the Science Center, to provide floor-by-floor guidance to specific classrooms, offices, or labs. This would significantly reduce the "last-mile" confusion students face once they enter a large building.

Another promising direction involves the integration of Augmented Reality (AR) technology. By leveraging the camera and compass sensors, a future module could overlay digital directional arrows onto the real-world view of the campus path. This "Live View" feature would provide an intuitive, immersive wayfinding experience, particularly useful for visitors who struggle to interpret 2D top-down maps. Additionally, implementing Real-Time Crowd Monitoring using anonymous location data could help students avoid congested areas, such as a full cafeteria during lunch hours, by suggesting less crowded alternatives.

To further personalize the user experience, future iterations will focus on Academic Calendar Integration. By connecting to the university's student portal API, the application could automatically pull a student's class schedule and highlight the exact building they need to be in for their next lecture. This feature would transform the app from a passive directory into an active daily assistant. Furthermore, accessibility can be improved by introducing Multi-Language Support and a system-wide Dark Theme, ensuring the application serves the diverse needs of the international student body and remains comfortable to use during evening classes.

Finally, the system infrastructure could be scaled to support Events and News integration. A dynamic "Events Layer" on the map could highlight temporary venues for graduation, orientation, or club activities, complete with navigation to specific tents or stalls. With these enhancements, the USIU Campus Navigator is well-positioned to evolve from a static navigation tool into a comprehensive, intelligent campus companion that adapts to the dynamic nature of university life.

# ABSTRACT

Navigating large university campuses can be a significant challenge for students, staff, and visitors, particularly in identifying the locations of lecture halls, offices, and essential service points. This project proposes the design and development of a Campus Navigation and Mapping Application for United States International University – Africa (USIU-A). The primary objective of the system is to provide a user-friendly mobile solution that enables accurate indoor and outdoor navigation within the campus while offering detailed information about key facilities.

The proposed system will integrate Firebase Firestore for backend data management and the Google Maps API for interactive mapping and routing functionalities. Users will be able to search for buildings, retrieve relevant details, and receive guided routes from their current location to the desired destination. To enhance usability, the application will incorporate a clean and intuitive interface designed in Android Studio, while also ensuring performance, scalability, and data security.

A phased methodology will be adopted in the development of this system, including research, requirements gathering, design, implementation, testing, evaluation, deployment, and documentation. The expected outcome is a reliable and efficient navigation tool that improves accessibility, reduces time wastage, and enhances the overall campus experience for students, staff, and visitors.

This project not only addresses a practical navigation problem but also contributes to the broader vision of building smart campuses by leveraging modern mobile technologies and cloud-based services.

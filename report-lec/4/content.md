# CHAPTER FOUR: PROPOSED PROJECT

The development of any software system requires a structured and systematic approach that ensures the final product meets both user needs and technical requirements. One of the most effective ways of achieving this is by organizing the project into distinct phases that guide the progression of work from the initial idea to the final deployment. Each phase plays a critical role in reducing risks, clarifying objectives, and ensuring that the project maintains focus throughout its lifecycle.

For the proposed campus navigation system at United States International University – Africa (USIU-A), the project has been divided into a series of phases that align with standard software development practices while also reflecting the unique requirements of this study. These phases provide a step-by-step pathway that begins with research and requirements analysis, advances through design and implementation, and concludes with testing, evaluation, deployment, and documentation.

The purpose of this chapter is to present these proposed phases in detail, highlighting the activities carried out in each stage, their significance, and the expected outcomes. By structuring the project into clear phases, it becomes possible to manage resources effectively, monitor progress, and ensure that the final system is robust, user-friendly, and sustainable.

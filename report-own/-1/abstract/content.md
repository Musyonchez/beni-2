# ABSTRACT

Managing food orders in a busy university cafeteria presents a significant operational challenge, particularly during peak hours between lectures when hundreds of students compete for limited counter space and preparation time. This project proposes the design and development of a Cafeteria Ordering System for United States International University – Africa (USIU-A). The primary objective of the system is to provide a mobile solution that enables students to browse the cafeteria menu, place orders remotely, and receive real-time status updates — eliminating the need to physically queue at the counter.

The proposed system will be developed as a native Android application using Java and XML, with Firebase Firestore as the cloud-based real-time database, Firebase Authentication for user management, and Firebase Cloud Messaging for push notifications. The application introduces an in-app wallet that allows students to hold a balance and settle orders without cash on every visit. A Smart Pre-order feature enables students to schedule meals in advance around their lecture timetable, including recurring orders by day of the week, with automated wallet deduction enforced by Firebase Cloud Functions at configurable meal cut-off times. An estimated wait time indicator, derived from the current order queue depth, gives students visibility into how long their order will take before committing to it.

A phased methodology will be adopted, encompassing research and requirements gathering, system design, implementation, testing and evaluation, and documentation. The expected outcome is a reliable and efficient ordering platform that reduces peak-hour congestion, improves the dining experience for students, and gives cafeteria staff real-time demand visibility — contributing to the broader goal of a smarter, more connected campus at USIU-A.

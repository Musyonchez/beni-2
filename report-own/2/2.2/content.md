# 2.2 Global Perspective

Globally, food ordering technology has matured significantly over the past two decades, driven by the widespread adoption of smartphones and cloud-based services. Mobile applications such as those developed by McDonald's, Starbucks, and Uber Eats have demonstrated that customers are willing to shift order placement entirely to their devices, provided the experience is fast, reliable, and integrated with a payment mechanism. These platforms introduced concepts — real-time order tracking, push notifications, and digital wallets — that are now considered standard in commercial food service.

Within universities, several institutions in North America and Asia have applied these same principles to their dining services, developing dedicated mobile platforms that allow students to browse menus, place orders, and receive notifications when their meal is ready. Research into these deployments consistently shows reductions in peak-hour congestion, improved student satisfaction, and better demand visibility for cafeteria staff. Despite this progress, most solutions at the global level are deeply integrated with institutional infrastructure and are not easily replicated without significant technical and financial investment.

# 2.2.2 NUS uNivUS (National University of Singapore, Singapore)

The National University of Singapore (NUS) developed uNivUS, a campus-wide mobile application that consolidates a range of student services, including a canteen ordering and queue management system. Through the food ordering module, students can browse stalls across NUS canteens, place orders remotely, and receive estimated wait times before deciding to walk to the collection point. The platform also integrates digital payment through the NUS student card and linked payment methods, removing the need for cash at the counter. Because NUS operates a large, geographically spread campus with multiple canteen facilities, the ability to check queue status and order ahead has a measurable impact on how students plan their meal breaks around lectures and tutorials.

The NUS uNivUS system stands out among global university food service platforms for combining ordering, queue visibility, and digital payment within a single mobile interface. The queue status feature is directly relevant to the problem this project addresses — students at USIU-A cannot currently estimate their wait before committing their break time to the cafeteria. The digital payment integration through a linked campus card also demonstrates a practical cashless model that does not require a third-party payment gateway at the point of service.

The system's limitations stem primarily from its dependence on NUS infrastructure. The digital payment layer relies on the NUS student card ecosystem, and the order routing depends on direct integration with individual canteen stall operators — both of which require institutional coordination beyond the scope of a student project. Furthermore, NUS uNivUS does not support recurring scheduled orders; students must place each order individually. For the USIU Cafeteria Ordering System, the NUS model directly informs the queue visibility and digital wallet features, while the absence of scheduled pre-ordering in the NUS platform underscores an opportunity for this project to go further.

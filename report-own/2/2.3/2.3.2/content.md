# 2.3.2 UNILAG Cafeteria System (University of Lagos, Nigeria)

The University of Lagos (UNILAG) has introduced a cashless payment mechanism in its cafeteria operations through a student ID card system with an embedded NFC-based payment function. Students load credit onto their student card and tap it at the cafeteria counter to pay for meals, eliminating the need to handle cash at the point of service. Digital menu boards display the day's offerings and prices, providing basic menu visibility without requiring a mobile device. The system represents a meaningful step toward modernising food service in a large Nigerian university and has been well received for reducing transaction times at the counter.

The UNILAG system demonstrates that cashless payment in an institutional cafeteria is both technically achievable and operationally beneficial in the African university context. The reduction in cash handling at the counter has practical value for both students and staff. The digital menu board, while static, addresses the basic menu visibility problem by ensuring students can see what is available from a distance.

However, the system stops well short of mobile ordering. Students still queue physically at the counter, and food preparation only begins when a student is served. There is no mobile interface, no pre-ordering, no order status tracking, and no advance scheduling. The NFC card payment, while functional, requires students to visit a physical top-up point rather than managing their balance through an app. For the USIU Cafeteria Ordering System, UNILAG validates the value of cashless payment in this context while illustrating that payment modernisation alone does not solve the queuing problem — a mobile-first approach that moves order placement off the counter entirely is required.

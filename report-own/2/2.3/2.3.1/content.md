# 2.3.1 UCT Dining App (University of Cape Town, South Africa)

The University of Cape Town (UCT) operates a dedicated mobile dining application that manages student access to campus dining halls through a QR code and meal plan system. Each student is issued a personal QR code linked to their student account, which is presented at the dining hall entrance to be scanned for admission or to redeem a meal voucher. The app's Settings section displays the student's personal details — including their residence hall and account status — alongside a meal plan summary indicating which meals (Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner) are covered under their plan. The bottom navigation provides quick access to Home, Vouchers, Scan, Show, and Settings, keeping all dining functions within a single interface.

The UCT Dining App demonstrates a mature institutional dining model built around identity-linked access control and structured meal plans. The QR code mechanism eliminates the need for a physical student card at every dining transaction, while the meal plan display gives students clear visibility of their covered meals. The voucher system adds flexibility for ad-hoc purchases beyond the standard plan.

The system's focus is primarily on access and entitlement management rather than advance ordering or queue reduction. Students present their QR code on arrival rather than placing orders in advance, meaning the app does not address peak-hour queuing directly. There is no pre-ordering, estimated wait time, or push notification for order readiness. For the USIU Cafeteria Ordering System, the UCT model informs the importance of linking a student's identity to their dining balance, while highlighting pre-ordering, real-time order tracking, and push notifications as features that a more transactional platform can deliver.

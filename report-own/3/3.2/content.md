# 3.2 General Aims and Objectives

The general aim of this project is to design, develop, and evaluate a mobile-based cafeteria ordering system specifically for United States International University – Africa (USIU-A). This initiative seeks to directly address the documented inefficiencies faced by students and cafeteria staff in the current manual ordering process — eliminating physical queuing, providing menu visibility before arrival, enabling advance meal scheduling, and introducing a cashless in-app wallet for frictionless payment. By replacing the existing counter-based, cash-only process with a mobile-first platform, the project aims to reduce peak-hour congestion, improve the dining experience for students, and give cafeteria staff the real-time demand visibility they currently lack. Ultimately, this endeavour contributes to the broader goal of building a smarter, more connected campus at USIU-A.

A comprehensive review of existing literature will be conducted to establish a strong theoretical and practical foundation for this project. This survey will critically analyse prior research and existing implementations of food ordering and cafeteria management systems, with a specific focus on their application within educational institutions. It will evaluate the approaches taken by global, regional, and local systems, assess their respective strengths and limitations, and identify the usability principles and technical patterns most relevant to a university cafeteria context. This process will ensure the project is informed by established practice and will clearly define the gap that the proposed USIU-A system is designed to fill.

The design and development of the application will be guided by a structured architectural framework to ensure robustness, usability, and data integrity. The system will be developed as a native Android application, with Firebase Firestore as the cloud-based real-time database, Firebase Authentication for user management, and Firebase Cloud Messaging for push notifications. Key functionalities to be implemented include a browsable and searchable menu, a shopping cart with wallet or cash payment selection, real-time order status tracking with estimated wait time, a Smart Pre-order feature with recurring scheduling and automated cut-off processing via Firebase Cloud Functions, and role-based access for students and cafeteria staff within a single application. The design process will prioritise a user-centred approach, ensuring the system is tailored to the specific operational context of the USIU-A cafeteria.

The final phase of the project will involve rigorous testing and evaluation of the developed prototype to validate its effectiveness and readiness for use. Testing will encompass both technical performance and user acceptance. Technically, the application will be assessed for order flow reliability, real-time synchronisation speed, wallet transaction atomicity, pre-order cut-off accuracy, and performance under load. For user-centric evaluation, representative students and cafeteria staff will be recruited to participate in acceptance testing and provide feedback on ease of use, reliability, and overall satisfaction. The insights gained from this evaluation will be used to demonstrate the extent to which the system meets its core objective of transforming the USIU-A cafeteria experience.

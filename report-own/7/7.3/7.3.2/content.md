# 7.3.2 Peer Review Session

In Stage 2, three peers from USIU-Africa participated in a structured review session. Two peers played the student role and one peer played the staff role, each using the application independently for approximately 15 minutes without developer guidance. Participants were given a brief verbal description of the app's purpose and then asked to explore it freely while the developer observed and noted feedback.

No functional failures were observed during the peer session. The following feedback was collected:

- *"Adding items and seeing the total change in the cart feels fast and natural."* — Student participant
- *"I wasn't sure what 'Pre-order' meant at first — a short tooltip or label would help."* — Student participant
- *"The notification when the order was ready was a nice touch — I would actually use this app."* — Student participant
- *"The wallet balance being visible in the cart before paying is very useful — you know immediately whether you have enough."* — Student participant

The staff participant navigated the StaffOrders screen without assistance and correctly identified the "Start Preparing" and "Mark Ready" buttons. The StaffWallet top-up flow was completed successfully on the first attempt.

The main usability observation from this stage was that the "Pre-orders" section label was not immediately self-explanatory to first-time users unfamiliar with the concept of advance scheduling. This is noted as a future enhancement — a short onboarding label or tooltip on first launch would improve discoverability — and is recorded as a minor UI refinement rather than a functional defect. Overall, the application was received positively, with participants expressing that the ordering flow was intuitive and that the real-time notification feature added significant value.

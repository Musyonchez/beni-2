# 4.3.1 Phase One: Research, Requirements Gathering, And System Design

## i. Research

**Objective:** To understand the cafeteria ordering problem at USIU and review existing global and regional food ordering systems.

**Tasks:** Review global (MIT Dining, NUS uNivUS) and regional/local (UCT, UNILAG, UoN, Strathmore) systems; observe peak-hour cafeteria operations at USIU-A; conduct informal interviews with students and cafeteria staff.

**Deliverables:** Research findings report; identified gaps in current solutions.

**Timeline:** Week 1

---

## ii. Requirements Gathering

**Objective:** To define what the system should do.

**Tasks:** Identify functional requirements (menu browsing, ordering, wallet, pre-order, staff management) and non-functional requirements (real-time sync speed, wallet atomicity, security rules, performance).

**Deliverables:** Documented functional and non-functional requirements; 20 test cases defined.

**Timeline:** Week 2

---

## iii. Proposal

**Objective:** To consolidate research and requirements into a structured plan.

**Tasks:** Draft abstract, background, literature review, aims, methodology, and project plan tailored to the USIU cafeteria context.

**Deliverables:** Completed and approved project proposal.

**Timeline:** Week 3

---

## iv. System Architecture and Design

**Objective:** To design the system structure and workflows.

**Tasks:** Develop use case diagrams, ERDs, DFDs (Level 0 and Level 1), flowcharts (regular order and pre-order cut-off), class diagram, wireframes for all 5 screens, and Firestore security rules specification.

**Deliverables:** Complete system design document with all diagrams and wireframes.

**Timeline:** Week 4
